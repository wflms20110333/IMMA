How does site tracking affect my privacy?

The short answer: Although we do track which sites you visit, we can't see that information.

To preserve your privacy, we keep information locally on your own computer whenever possible.

The long answer: Processing of your visited sites is done locally on your device.

And even locally, we don't store a record of which sites you've visited in the past.

We only measure which sites you currently have open.

Your predicted mood, along with your Browserbug's settings, is anonymously sent to our server so we can choose the best message for you.

We don't record, analyze, or otherwise use any server interactions.

(Partly because we're too lazy, but also because we believe that it would be an invasion of your privacy.)

We profit through users' purchases of premium features, and through donations. (Servers are expensive, please help us offset costs!)

If we ever include advertisements, we won't ever try to share or sell your information to those third parties.

And in general, we don't share or sell your data. (It's not like we have much data anyway...)

The even longer answer: Want more details? Check out our Privacy Policy!

Feel free to read it to learn more about how we interact with data!
